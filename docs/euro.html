EURO

Euro, the official currency in the Eurozone, was introduced as a virtual currency in 1999 and later as physical in 2002. It is the single currency shared by 19 of the European Unionâ€™s Member States of Euro Area. Although European Central Bank (ECB) manages Euro, the fiscal policy (public revenue and expenditure) are in the hands of individual national authorities. The single currency market throughout the euro zone not only makes traveling across the countries easier but also helps the member country to keep their economy sound and stable. This situation removes currency exchange cost, smooth international trade and consequently gives them more powerful voice in the world. A stable economy and larger area protects euro zone from external economic fluctuations, instability in currency market and unpredictable rise in oil prices.(euro2015ecb)
